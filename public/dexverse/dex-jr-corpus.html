02 Ingestion Philosophy
Broad ingestion. Controlled retrieval weighting.
The corpus does not gatekeep at ingestion time. Metadata is the governance layer — not the ingestion gate. A wide archive at low weight is better than a narrow archive at high weight. The 271K-chunk ddl_archive at 0.65 provides necessary historical context that a filtered archive would have permanently excluded.

Retrieval precision comes from the tier system, the weight table, and the query router — not from what you chose not to ingest. When uncertain whether something belongs in the corpus, ingest it into the archive tier and let the weights handle the rest.

The one exception is dex_dave — the operator voice corpus. It is subject to a hard ingestion gate until the query router is live, because unrouted operator voice at 0.90 weight in the default pool introduces confabulation risk for governance queries. The gate is architectural, not a statement about the corpus's value.

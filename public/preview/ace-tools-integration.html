How all the tools fit together

ACE is one piece. Here's the whole ecosystem — what each tool does, how they connect, and why it matters that Dave and Clayton are building toward the same thing.

The Big Analogy

It's like opening a restaurant

Every tool in the system has a role, like every person in a restaurant has a job. None of them work alone. Together, they turn ingredients into a meal.

The Restaurant 🍽️

📋

The Filing Cabinet

ACE

Stores every recipe, every supplier contact, every menu version. Labeled so anyone can find anything. Without this, you lose track of what you've already figured out.

🤝

The Greeter

MindFrame

Meets new staff on day one. Figures out how they think, what they're good at, how they communicate. Builds their profile so the kitchen doesn't have to guess.

📐

The Build Process

DDL

When you want to add a new dish to the menu, DDL is the step-by-step process: define it, scope it, build it, test it, ship it. No winging it.

🏗️

The Operations Manual

DexOS

The rules for how the whole restaurant runs. Who does what, how things are named, what quality standards look like. The operating system underneath everything.

👨‍🍳👩‍🍳

The Tasting Panel

Council

Multiple expert opinions on every important decision. Not one chef deciding alone — a panel of specialists who each taste the dish and give honest feedback.

🔍

The Health Inspector

BlindSpot

Finds the problems you don't know you have. Checks every corner of the kitchen for things that could go wrong before they do. Fills gaps proactively.

🤖

The Kitchen Automation

Auto-Council

Instead of manually asking each expert one at a time, this sends the dish to all of them at once and collects their feedback automatically. Saves hours.

📚

The Knowledge System

Clayton's Platform

Reads every document in the restaurant — menus, policies, training manuals — clusters them by topic, and when someone asks a question it can't answer, it flags it so a manager can fill the gap.

🍎 The ELI5

Dave built the filing system, the hiring process, the build process, the operations manual, the expert panel, and the safety inspector. Clayton built the smart library that reads everything and finds what's missing. Together they make a restaurant where nothing gets lost, nothing gets missed, and new staff can get up to speed on day one.

Tool by Tool

What each one actually does

In plain English. No jargon. Promise.

📋

ACE

Artifact Continuity Engine

A labeling system for everything you create with AI. Every important document, decision, or framework gets a name, a type, a version, and a status. Stored as simple text files you can open anywhere.

📌 Like putting a label on every moving box when you move houses. "Kitchen — Plates — Box 3 of 5."
🤝

MindFrame

Persona Calibration

A 30-step process that helps AI understand how you think, communicate, and work. Instead of starting from scratch, the AI gets a profile of you so it can match your style from the first message.

📌 Like filling out a detailed intake form at a new doctor's office — but for your thinking style, not your medical history.
📐

DDL

Dropdown Logistics — Build Methodology

An 8-step process for building anything: define the intent, set the scope, build it, test it, ship it, document it. Prevents "I had a great idea but it went nowhere" syndrome.

📌 Like a recipe. You don't just throw ingredients in a pan — you follow steps, in order, and the dish comes out right.
🏗️

DexOS

The Operating System

The master rule book. How things are named, how quality is checked, how different AI models are coordinated. Think of it as the governance layer that keeps everything consistent.

📌 Like a franchise manual. Every location runs differently, but the standards are the same everywhere.
👨‍🍳

The Council

Multi-Model Analysis

Instead of asking one AI for an answer, you ask 8–10 different ones the same question. They all respond independently. Where they agree = high confidence. Where they disagree = worth investigating.

📌 Like getting a second opinion from multiple doctors. If 8 out of 10 say the same thing, you can trust that answer.
🔍

BlindSpot

Gap Detection

A system that looks at what you know and finds what you're missing. Instead of waiting for a problem to show up, it actively hunts for gaps in your knowledge or strategy.

📌 Like a home inspector who finds the crack in the foundation before you buy the house.
🤖

Auto-Council

Dave + Clayton's Joint Project

Right now, Dave sends the same question to each AI model one at a time and manually collects responses. Auto-Council automates that — send once, collect from all, compare automatically.

📌 Like sending one email to 10 people instead of writing 10 separate emails with the same question.
📚

Clayton's Knowledge Platform

Enterprise Knowledge Management

Feed it your company's documents — manuals, policies, SOPs. It reads them all, organizes them into clusters, and when employees ask questions, it either answers from existing knowledge or flags the gap for a manager to fill.

📌 Like a librarian who's read every book in the library and can either answer your question or tell you "we don't have a book on that yet — want me to flag it?"
How It All Flows

The journey of an idea, start to finish

Here's what happens when someone has an idea and the full system is running:

From Idea → Governed Knowledge

Every step has a tool. No step is skipped.

1

Someone shows up

MindFrame

Figures out how they think, what they need, and builds their profile. First conversation feels like tenth conversation.

2

They want to build something

DDL

Define intent → set scope → build → test → ship → document. No ambiguity about what "done" looks like.

3

They need expert feedback

Council → Auto-Council

Send the work to multiple AI models. Get independent analysis. Find where they agree and where they don't. Auto-Council does this automatically.

4

Check for blind spots

BlindSpot

What are we missing? What haven't we considered? What question haven't we asked? Find the gaps before they become problems.

5

Save everything that matters

ACE

Label it. Version it. File it. So the next time anyone works on this — even a different AI, even months later — they can pick up right where you left off.

6

Scale it to a whole organization

Clayton's Platform + DexOS

Everything that was just built for one person now works for a company. Documents get ingested, clustered, searchable. Questions get routed. Gaps get flagged. Knowledge grows automatically.

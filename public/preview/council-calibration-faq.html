A
System Identity & Scope
What is DexOS trying to achieve?
DexOS preserves clarity and intent across time, tools, and contributors — so work stays resumable, comparable, and shippable.
Council consensus: Approved as crisp, aligned mission statement.
What is DexOS in practical terms?
DexOS is a behavior-first coordination framework designed to keep long-running, multi-model work coherent, predictable, and resilient. It is not an agent, persona, or autonomous decision-maker.
What problem does DexOS explicitly solve?
DexOS addresses drift, loss of intent, silent assumption creep, and breakdowns in coordination across time, tools, and models.
What does DexOS refuse to be?
It is not a persona, not an agent, not a narrative system, and not autonomous.
Council consensus: Approved as necessary boundary; prevents persona drift.
B
Architecture & Boundaries
Why is behavior defined before structure?
Structure without behavioral clarity produces brittle systems. DexOS ensures behavior is understood before enforcement is applied.
How do MindFrame, Runtime, Relay, and DexCity differ?
MindFrame governs behavior and modes. Runtime enforces structure. Relay manages communication and packet integrity. DexCity provides a conceptual and visual map of behavior and risk.
Why is DexCity not enforcement?
DexCity exists to make the system legible. Enforcement belongs exclusively to Runtime and MindFrame.
What is a "shared anchor"?
Anchor = Scope + Constraints + Non-goals + Pass/Fail test. All anchors must be documented in-thread.
Council convergence: "Anchor must be testable, not vibe." Explicit persistence requirement requested by LeChat.
C
Council Role
What is the Council's function?
The Council provides perspective diversity, failure detection, and decision support through selective, phase-appropriate intervention.
When should a Council member intervene?
When work enters a failure-prone zone outside the current lead's strengths or when assumptions go unexamined.
What does good Council participation look like?
Scoped, timely contributions that increase clarity or safety without introducing unnecessary complexity.
D
Decision Thresholds
When should work stop?
Stop solutioning when intent, success criteria, or constraints can't be stated plainly enough to guide the next move. Then switch tasks: clarify the decision, tighten the anchor, or escalate.
Council note: Keeps "stop" as task-switch, not halt. Praised as momentum-safe.
When is a v1 ready?
When scope is explicit, failure modes are bounded, and validation is pass/fail with one stated test.
Council convergence: Demanded bounded failure + one test; avoids "v1 by vibes."
When does polish become risk?
When polish hides unresolved decisions or delays exposure to failure.
What signals overbuilding?
Future-proofing replaces present requirements, v2 logic leaks into v1 work, or elegance is prioritized over correctness.
E
Drift, Failure & Recovery
How do we recognize drift?
When outputs feel smooth but can't be tested, or when narrative replaces artifacts.
Council-wide drift signature: "Narrative replacing artifacts."
How should ambiguity be handled?
Name it, bound it, then proceed with the smallest reversible step. Reversible = the step does not become downstream canon if it's wrong.
Council convergence: Ambiguity allowed only if bounded + reversible.
When should escalation occur?
  • The work exits the safe zone of the current lead
  • Ambiguity blocks the pass/fail test
  • The same ambiguity blocks progress 3+ times
  • Contributors produce incompatible plans for the same anchor
  • Ambiguity touches the export contract or risks irreversible damage
Council note: Countable triggers requested across council; prevents infinite loops.
How do we recover momentum?
Collapse to one decision, one anchor, and one next action.
Council pattern: Repeated recovery move across members; keeps output artifact-driven.
F
Routing & Handoffs
How should leadership rotate?
Leadership follows the bottleneck and the shape of the problem, not convenience.
Council agreement: Routing follows "problem shape," not availability.
What causes most failures?
Proceeding without a shared anchor — especially around scope, constraints, non-goals, or "done."
Council note: Primary failure mode repeated by multiple reviewers.
How should disagreement be handled?
By identifying whether the conflict concerns intent, constraints, or risk tolerance.
G
Meta
Why capture this calibration?
To create a behavioral baseline that survives time gaps and model changes.
How should this document be used?
As a reference, not a rulebook.
What should not be done with this material?
It should not be weaponized, treated as doctrine, or used to override context.

Platform reality: Assume tooling, memory, and persistence vary by model and thread. Anchors must be written, not implied.

Council flagged: Persistence/tool variance as #1 silent constraint.

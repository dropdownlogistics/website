🌿 The Philosophy 🌿
01
You are not a religion.

Veganism as identity politics has failed everyone who ever tried it at a family dinner. Strategan is not an identity. It is an operating system. You eat with intention, not with a manifesto.

02
The context matters more than the category.

Factory-farmed anything is a skip. A heritage-breed lamb chop from a farm you trust is not an ethical failure. It is a strategic choice. Read the room. Know your source.

03
Optimization over elimination.

The goal is to eat more plants, fewer things that required suffering to produce, and zero things that are just bad because they're convenient. That's the whole framework.

04
The Michelin Clause.

If a Michelin-star chef put it in front of you, and they sourced it with intention — eat it. Refusing on principle in that context isn't integrity. It's waste dressed up as virtue.

⬡ The Decision Framework ⬡

Every food decision runs through the same filter. Not "is this vegan?" — that's a binary that collapses under any real-world pressure. The Strategan filter is: is this intentional?

You eat plants by default. You eat animals by exception. You make the exception count.

🌿 Eat / Skip 🌿
✓ Strategic Eats
  • All vegetables — always, no exception, this is the whole point
  • Legumes, grains, seeds — default fuel, no apology needed
  • Heritage / pasture-raised meat — sourced, intentional, earned
  • Wild-caught fish — context matters, source matters
  • Anything a trusted chef sourced — Michelin Clause, full stop
  • Fermented everything — your roommate who farms knows why
  • Eggs from birds you've met — or at least from birds that saw the sun
✗ Strategic Skips
  • Factory-farmed anything — not a strategic exception, just a bad habit
  • Ultra-processed "vegan" products — the label doesn't make it food
  • Convenience meat — fast food, gas station, parking lot decisions
  • Dairy from operations you can't picture — if you don't know, skip
  • Dogma — refusing food on principle at someone's table is its own kind of bad
  • Virtue signaling disguised as diet — no one is impressed, eat your vegetables
